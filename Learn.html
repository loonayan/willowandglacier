What is the Willow Project?

The Willow Project is a non-profit organization that helps to preserve and protect the natural environment of the Pacific Northwest region of the United States. It was founded in 2005 by a group of local activists who were concerned about the impact of human activity on the area's forests, rivers, and wildlife.

What does the Willow Project do?

The Willow Project is involved in a wide range of activities, including:

  • Planting trees and restoring damaged forests
  • Cleaning up rivers and streams
  • Protecting endangered species
  • Educating the public about environmental issues
  • Advocating for stronger environmental policies

How can you get involved?

If you're interested in supporting the Willow Project, there are many ways you can get involved:

  • Volunteering for tree-planting and restoration projects
  • Participating in river clean-up events
  • Donating money to support the organization's work
  • Spreading the word about the Willow Project and its mission
